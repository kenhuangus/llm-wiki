## Claims
- Pretrained Vision Transformers (ViTs) such as DINOv2 and MAE provide generic image features that can be applied to a variety of downstream tasks such as retrieval, classification, and segmentation. (Confidence: 0.98, Source: [[384b65ef]])
- Such representations tend to focus on the most salient visual cues in the image, with no way to direct them toward less prominent concepts of interest. (Confidence: 0.95, Source: [[384b65ef]])
- Multimodal LLMs can be guided with textual prompts, but the resulting representations tend to be language-centric and lose their effectiveness for generic visual tasks. (Confidence: 0.93, Source: [[384b65ef]])
- We introduce Steerable Visual Representations, a new class of visual representations, whose global and local features can be steered with natural language. (Confidence: 0.97, Source: [[384b65ef]])
- We inject text directly into the layers of the visual encoder (early fusion) via lightweight cross-attention. (Confidence: 0.96, Source: [[384b65ef]])
- Our method also matches or outperforms dedicated approaches on anomaly detection and personalized object discrimination, exhibiting zero-shot generalization to out-of-distribution tasks. (Confidence: 0.94, Source: [[384b65ef]])

## Claims
- Recent advances in video diffusion have enabled the development of "world models" capable of simulating interactive environments. (Confidence: 0.95, Source: [[8ed8a7e1]])
- These models are largely restricted to single-agent settings, failing to control multiple agents simultaneously in a scene. (Confidence: 0.98, Source: [[8ed8a7e1]])
- ActionParty is an action controllable multi-subject world model for generative video games. (Confidence: 0.96, Source: [[8ed8a7e1]])
- It introduces subject state tokens, i.e. latent variables that persistently capture the state of each subject in the scene. (Confidence: 0.97, Source: [[8ed8a7e1]])
- By jointly modeling state tokens and video latents with a spatial biasing mechanism, we disentangle global video frame rendering from individual action-controlled subject updates. (Confidence: 0.95, Source: [[8ed8a7e1]])
- We evaluate ActionParty on the Melting Pot benchmark, demonstrating the first video world model capable of controlling up to seven players simultaneously across 46 diverse environments. (Confidence: 0.98, Source: [[8ed8a7e1]])
- Our results show significant improvements in action-following accuracy and identity consistency, while enabling robust autoregressive tracking of subjects through complex interactions. (Confidence: 0.95, Source: [[8ed8a7e1]])
